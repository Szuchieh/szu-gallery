Ava

End to End Fitness App Project

Overview

Ava
A device that gathers data from workout machines, and provides data analysis to the trainer and their clients
Goal
Design a tablet interface for personal trainers to create customized workouts for clients
My Role
  • Usability testing
  • UX analysis
  • Solution synthesis
  • Lo-fi prototyping
  • Style guide
  • Hi-fi prototyping

AVA was a fun and challenging project because we built it from scratch. Based on the concept the client had, we narrowed down our focus to design an interface for trainers to to create customized workouts for their clients. During the process I learned about the mental model of personal trainers by conducting user interviews and tests, and the importance of converging ideas while finishing the full resolution designs. When we delivered the final prototype, the client was very pleased with our final results.

Define Problem

In order to better understand a personal trainer’s pain points, we went to the local gym and interviewed five trainers. Then we synthesized to three insights.

Traditional ways to program workouts limit trainers to a small client base.
Manually programming workouts is time intensive and unpaid.
Workout templates are stored in their heads leading to loss of information.
“I spend so much time programming workouts. The other day I had six clients coming up for renewal of their programs. It took me all day long – about eight hours – and I only finished four of them.”

-Samar, Personal Trainer @ Equinox

Personas

Based on our understanding from research phase, we identified two types of users for the new product. Brock, who represented the trainers and Erica, who represented the clients. These personas guided our design decisions.

Brock, 28

Primary Persona
Demographics
Location: San Francisco
Career: Trainer at Equinox
Income: $55k/year

Behaviors
  • Spends eight hours during the weekend to program workouts for new clients
  • Spends three hours on weekends to adjust program when clients change their workout goals
  • Uses excel spreadsheets to track client’s progress

Motivations
To be able to have more clients, so he can increase his income

Erica, 32

Secondary Persona
Demographics
Location: San Francisco
Career: Program Manager at UCSF
Income: $83k/year

Behaviors
  • Works out five to six times per week
  • Resistance training three to four times per week
  • High caloric intake
  • Uses Fitbit to track her workout progress

Motivations
To set a good example to her family, and to prove that when a woman becomes a mother, she can still stay fit

Ideation Round One

Taking our research insights into consideration, we first did a task flow to help us gain a sense of the scope of this product. Then we did several rounds of sketching and brainstorming to design the features we needed.

Lo-fi Prototype & Validation

We converged our ideas into a lo-fi prototype design, and then conducted useability tests with five personal trainers.

Ideation Round Two

Based on the user feedback, we realized this product had several layers including the template view, week view, and workout view. Each view has its own information to be edited. It was a challenge to make this system easy to use and navigate, and we also needed to make it flow with a trainer’s mental model. So, we went back to white board for more iterations and brainstorming.

Final Prototype

After several rounds of brainstorming and ideation, we converged our ideas into the final prototype.

Results

100% of users easily built workout templates

100% users liked the breadcrumbs and overall navigation of the app

90% of users easily understood how to adjust sets, reps, and rest time

Overview

Chime
A mobile bank that eliminates unnecessary fees and focuses on helping millennials to have a healthier financial life
Goal
Redesign of an Android hompage to provide a positive experience for users
My Role
  • Usability testing
  • UX analysis
  • Presentation

This was a research heavy project that furthered my undersanding of user interviews, usability tests, and A/B testing. Our client was happy with the insights and direction we provided. I learned that when it comes to designing fin-tech apps, there are a unique set of challenges due to people’s proclivity to keep their information private, pre-existing trust relationships with their current banks, or their financial management habits.

Define Problem

We tested the original Chime product with five users.

The top five pain points were:

4/5 users were unsure what spending balance meant

3/5 users felt unclear about Chime’s security

3/5 users were not sure what roundups were

3/5 users felt that the savings outlook graph was unclear

3/5 users thought that there was too much information on the homepage

Ideation Round One

Based on the user pain points we gathered with the original design, we first defined three solutions, then restructured the homepage sections.

A clean home page that provides useful financial information
Clear graph for savings outlook
Use more descriptive language

A/B Testing

We developed two prototypes that focused on different values to validate.



Version A

For version A the account summary provided users how much a they can spend, and the savings outlook provided a weekly savings graph.



Version B

For version B, the account summary provided detailed information of each account and the savings outlook monthly report showed the projected savings for next month.
